🪑 Three-Legged Stool

Motive (WHY)

Underlying grievance behind a Person of Concern's behavior.

  • What is driving the grievance?
  • Why this target?
  • Why now?

Intent (WHAT)

The conscious objective to achieve a certain result.

  • What outcome does the subject want?
  • Has intent been communicated?
  • Is intent increasing?

Ability (HOW)

Capacity, resources, and physical/mental capability to carry out an act of violence.

  • Access to the target?
  • Access to weapons or means?
  • Skills or experience?
  • Physical/mental capability?
Without all three legs, the stool cannot stand.
🚦 Levels of Concern

● Low

  • General grievances
  • No target identified
  • No concerning behaviors

● Moderate

  • Target identified
  • Fixation
  • Leakage
  • Escalating rhetoric
  • Growing sense of injustice

● Elevated

  • Surveillance
  • Research / Planning
  • Weapon acquisition
  • Significant behavioral changes
  • Increasing commitment

● High

  • Operational planning
  • Security probing
  • Dry runs
  • Travel toward target
  • Pre-attack preparation
  • Resource acquisition
⚠️ Risk Enhancers & Reducers

Risk Enhancers

  • Targeted grievance
  • Fixation
  • Leakage
  • Identification with previous attackers
  • Moral outrage
  • Major life stressor
  • Social isolation
  • Substance abuse
  • Domestic violence history
  • Weapon acquisition
  • Surveillance
  • Security probing
  • Triggering event
  • Violent rehearsal
  • Escalating communications

Risk Reducers

  • Family support
  • Positive relationships
  • Stable employment
  • Mental health treatment
  • Means reduction
  • Protective monitoring
  • Community involvement
  • Future orientation
  • Passage of time
❓ Investigative Questions

Key Questions

  • What changed?
  • Why now?
  • Why this target?
  • What evidence supports Motive?
  • What evidence supports Intent?
  • What evidence supports Ability?
  • What interventions could reduce risk?
🎯 Mitigation Strategies

Reduce Risk

  • Reduce Motive
  • Reduce Intent
  • Reduce Ability
  • Increase protective factors
  • Reduce opportunity for violence
  • Continue assessment and reassessment
